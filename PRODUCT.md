# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

COOs, founders, and operators in Southeast Asia evaluating AI for their business. They are skeptical of hype, respect proof over claims, and have likely been burned by consultants selling theory. They scan fast, read slowly only when something earns it. They are not looking for a pitch — they are looking for evidence that the person on the other side of the page has actually done the work.

## Product Purpose

Personal brand site for YY Sng — COO of Laimi, AI operator across multiple ventures. The site exists to establish trust and signal competence to the specific audience above. Success is not traffic or impressions — it is the right person reaching out because the site proved, without saying it, that YY operates at a different level. The site updates itself via AI (Supabase + Claude Code). That fact is part of the proof.

## Brand Personality

Direct. Operator-first. Evidence over claims. Built, not designed.

Voice is field notes, not a keynote. Confident without performing confidence. The writing sounds like someone who has too much to do and chose to be precise rather than polished. Never motivational. Never a list of adjectives about vision.

## Anti-references

- SaaS landing page templates (Webflow showcase aesthetic)
- Motivational speaker / executive coach look (big inspirational quotes, stock-photo smiles)
- Personal brand coach aesthetic (pastel gradients, "authentic" cursive, follower counts)
- Inter font (too neutral, too default, signals nothing)
- Purple-to-blue gradients (AI startup cliché)
- Glassmorphism (frosted panels, blurred cards, fake depth)
- Cards-in-cards (nested surface layering)
- Startup hype tone ("We're reimagining…", "the future of…")
- Corporate consulting polish (McKinsey grid, navy + gold, serif authority)
- AI gimmick aesthetic (holographic, neon, robot imagery)

## Design Principles

1. **Show the work, not the claim.** Every design decision should surface evidence — real systems, real posts, real status. Never describe what YY does; show it in motion.
2. **Infrastructure over decoration.** Space, type, and structure carry meaning. Ornament is distrust. The page should feel like it was laid out by someone who understood the content, not styled by someone who needed it to look a certain way.
3. **Earn the scroll.** The above-the-fold earns one question: is this person worth my time? Every section below answers a follow-on question, not the same one again.
4. **Operator tempo.** No slow reveals, no cinematic entrances. Motion that exists for attention is noise. Motion that confirms state or guides eye is signal.
5. **Taste as proof.** The audience respects craft they can't name. Good typographic hierarchy, calibrated whitespace, and an unusual but justified color choice communicate taste without advertising it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum; target AAA for body text contrast
- Reduced motion respected (already implemented in codebase)
- No color-only affordances — status badges use both color and text label
- Southeast Asia primary audience — avoid idioms that don't translate
